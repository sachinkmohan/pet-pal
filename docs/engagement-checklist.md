# Engagement & Retention Checklist

Ordered by impact. Do these roughly in sequence — each one builds on the last.

  8 categories, ordered by impact:

  1. Make absence visible — degrading appearance, first-person "I miss you" notification
  2. Fix the egg problem — hatch in 2 sessions, animated crack, give it a face immediately
  3. Pochi has needs — time-sensitive needs with countdowns, not static mood labels
  4. Faster dopamine — celebrate every session, micro-evolutions between major stages, spendable coins
  5. First-person notifications — the single cheapest change with the biggest emotional return
  6. Feed mechanic matters — connect Mochi and Pochi, both visible on Home screen
  7. Return ritual — welcome back moment, lifetime "time together" counter
  8. Personality & social (longer term) — traits, dialogue, shareable milestone cards

  Start with #5 (notification copy) — it's one line of change per notification and will immediately make the app feel more alive. Then tackle #1 and #2 which
  fix the core loop.

---

## 1. Make absence visible (highest impact)

- [ ] Pochi's appearance degrades over time without sessions — eyes droop, colours desaturate, a small visual "wilt" after 24h with no session
- [ ] The Home screen shows a clear "Pochi is struggling" state (not just a mood label — an actual sad pose/animation)
- [ ] After 48h absence, Pochi sends a single push notification written in first person: *"I miss you 🥺"* — not from the app, from Pochi
- [ ] Recovery feels rewarding — returning after absence shows Pochi visibly perking up when you open the app

---

## 2. Fix the egg problem

- [ ] Egg hatches much faster — after 2 sessions, not 10
- [ ] Hatch is a moment: animated crack sequence, not just an emoji swap
- [ ] Give the egg a face immediately — even a sleeping face — so users feel something before it hatches
- [ ] Name the egg phase something warmer than "egg" — e.g. "Pochi is sleeping"

---

## 3. Pochi has needs, not just moods

- [ ] Replace mood states with time-sensitive *needs* — "Pochi wants to focus together", "Pochi is lonely", "Pochi is hungry"
- [ ] Each need has a countdown — if ignored for X hours it worsens visually
- [ ] Completing a focus session while Pochi is "lonely" gives a bonus reaction — Pochi jumps, sparkles, etc.
- [ ] Needs are surfaced on the Home screen prominently, not buried in a mood label

---

## 4. Faster, more frequent dopamine

- [ ] Add a small animation/celebration every single session completion — not just evolution milestones
- [ ] Show a session streak counter *on the Home screen* (not just in Stats) — make it the hero number
- [ ] Add micro-evolution steps between major stages — a small accessory, colour change, or expression change at 3, 7, 15 sessions
- [ ] Coin rewards should feel spendable — add something to spend coins on (accessories, backgrounds, pet name changes) even if simple

---

## 5. Reframe the notification copy (first person)

- [ ] Change all Pochi-related notifications from third person to first person
  - "Don't leave Pochi alone!" → "I'm waiting for you 🥺"
  - Session complete notification → "Thank you for staying with me! 🎉"
  - Streak at risk → "Please don't break our streak 😢"
- [ ] Add a "Pochi is bored" notification if no session has started by a user-configured time (e.g. 7 PM)

---

## 6. Make the feed mechanic matter

- [ ] Mochi (feed pet) and Pochi should feel connected — Mochi being well-fed should visibly make Pochi happier
- [ ] Show both pets on the Home screen so users see the full world, not just one pet
- [ ] Feed animation should be more satisfying — a chewing animation, a happy sound, a sparkle
- [ ] Missing a feed day should have a visual consequence on Mochi (droopy, sad)

---

## 7. Return ritual

- [ ] When user opens app after 12h+, show a "welcome back" moment — Pochi runs toward the camera, says something in first person
- [ ] After a completed session, show a "time together" counter — total hours spent with Pochi lifetime
- [ ] Weekly summary notification on Sunday: "We spent X hours together this week 💛"

---

## 8. Social proof & personality (longer term)

- [ ] Give Pochi randomised personality traits set during onboarding — "Pochi is adventurous / shy / energetic" — affects dialogue
- [ ] Pochi occasionally says something during the session countdown (shown on the timer screen)
- [ ] Share a "Pochi milestone" card when evolving — shareable image showing the pet and total focus hours

---

## Notes

- Every change here should make the user feel *responsible* for Pochi, not just rewarded by it
- Urgency without punishment: the pet never dies, but absence should feel genuinely uncomfortable
- First-person copy is non-negotiable — it's the single cheapest change with the highest emotional return
